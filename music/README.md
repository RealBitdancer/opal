# Demo music

Seven public-domain AdLib tracks in HSC AdLib Composer format (`.hsc`), bundled so the
example `player` has something to play out of the box.

## Source

The tracks come from **HSCDEMO3.EXE**, a 1992 AdLib music demo by **[Hannes Seifert](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hannes_Seifert)**
(author of the HSC format and the HSC AdLib Composer). Its title screen reads:

> AdLib Music Demo Release III. By Hannes Seifert 1992. Public Domain Version.

## Why only seven of the demo's 38 tracks

The original demo contains 38 tracks, but they are **not** all public domain. The demo lists
a copyright per track, and most are game soundtracks Seifert composed under contract, marked
`(C) 1991/1992 MAX Design` (the game *1869*, plus *Osiris*-themed pieces) or `(C) 1992 NEO`
(Neo Software). Those remain their publishers' property and are **excluded** here.

Only the tracks the demo itself marks **`independent`** or **`public domain`** are bundled.
They are Seifert's own work, covered by the demo's public-domain release. The per-track evidence
read directly from the running demo is recorded in [PUBLIC_DOMAIN_PROOF.txt](PUBLIC_DOMAIN_PROOF.txt).

- **Composer / author:** Hannes Seifert
- **Release:** Public Domain (1992)
- **Format:** HSC AdLib Composer (`.hsc`)
- **Original demo:** [HSCDEMO3.EXE on the Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/HSCDEMO3.EXE)

## Playing

The player identifies HSC files by their `.hsc` extension. Point it at any track (quote the
path since the names contain spaces):

    player "music/01 Shoot 'em up.hsc"
